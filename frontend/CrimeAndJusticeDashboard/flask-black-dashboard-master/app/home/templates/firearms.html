  • Firearm-related homicides declined 39%, from 18,253 in 2000 to 11,101 in 2019.
  • Nonfatal firearm crimes declined 69%, from 1.5 million victimizations in 1993 to 467,300 victimizations in 2019.
  • For both fatal and nonfatal firearm victimizations, the majority of the decline occurred during the 10-year period from 2000 to 2019.
  • Firearm violence accounted for about 70% of all homicides and less than 10% of all nonfatal violent crime from 2000 to 2019.
  • About 70% to 80% of firearm homicides and 90% of nonfatal firearm victimizations were committed with a handgun from 2000 to 2019.
  • From 2000 to 2019., males, blacks, and persons ages 18 to 24 had the highest rates of firearm homicide.
